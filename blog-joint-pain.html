Are You Suffering From Monsoon Joint Pain?

Monsoon is such a great weather to enjoy but many of us are unable to enjoy this season due to body and joint pain.so here are some facts to know about arthritis and joint pain.

During monsoon air pressure (Barometric pressure) is also a reason for joint pain, the lower air pressure pushes against the body. In turn, tissues expand and form a greater pressure on the joints. This pressure is so light and we don't actually notice it. The only sign of that pressure is the sensation of pain we feel.

Sedentary lifestyle and sitting for longer time in office leads to stiffness in body and joints. Mild stretching exercises and small walk during working hours can reverse this condition.

Another cause of monsoon pain is because of increased humidity. High humidity levels can make the blood thicker, causing increases blood pressure in the blood vessels, and makes the body specially heart work harder, to pump blood through the body. Humid days can cause the body to become dehydrated, which decreases the concentration of fluid around the joints and create more joint pain. Drinking more water or liquid can solve if problematic reason is less intake of water.

Ayurveda expert Dr.Prachi Gupta says increase in joint pain and stiffness during monsoon is very often and not only affecting old aged persons but younger ones are also suffering with this problem. The reason behind this pain is due to increase in vata dosha ,this vata creates more dryness in the joints leading to more friction, swelling and pain. Following vata pacifying regime can be helpful in this condition. Sitting in an air-conditioned environment aggravates the pain. Hence, make it a point to get up and walk a few steps every 1 hour. Hot oil massage over the painful joint will relax the joint and surrounding tissue. Patients already suffering from arthritis should be more conscious and careful for joint care during monsoon. Ayurveda is the only option which can guide you for proper diet and lifestyle to manage with this problem .
